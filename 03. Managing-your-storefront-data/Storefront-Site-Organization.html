Catalogs

There are two types of catalogs: the master catalog and the storefront catalog. Each storefront site needs to have one and only one storefront catalog assigned to it, which drives the navigation of the storefront.

Although this example uses only one catalog, it is possible to work with several catalogs at once by using a master catalog. Master catalogs contain the productsâ€™ data. A product that is owned by a master catalog can also be assigned to a storefront catalog so that it will display online.

You can share a master catalog between several sites. A set of products from a master catalog can be assigned to one storefront catalog, while a different set from the same master catalog can be assigned to another storefront catalog.

In this way, you can present different products to customers in two different countries while still managing the products centrally in one catalog.

The categories in a master catalog can be completely different from those in the storefront catalog. In fact, category trees cannot be shared among catalogs.

To determine if a catalog is a storefront or a master catalog, click on it or on the Edit link related to it. On the Catalog Edit page, the first section will indicate what kind of catalog it is.
